About Me

I'm a high school student at Pinghe High School in China, and my path into psychology started in an unexpected place: helping classmates work through tough moments and noticing how much better they felt afterward. That experience sparked a genuine curiosity about how people think, feel, and cope, and it's what led me to become captain of my school's psychology club and team lead for our psychology competitions. Outside of the classroom, I'm an only child who has learned to enjoy calm, quiet spaces — though I'll admit that being truly alone is something that still unsettles me. That contrast is part of why I'm drawn to psychology: I'm endlessly interested in the balance between solitude and connection, and in helping others find their own version of that balance.

Skills

My core strengths are therapy-style listening, logic, and critical thinking — a combination that lets me approach people's problems with both empathy and structure. I try to listen the way a good therapist does: without judgment, paying attention to what's underneath the words. At the same time, I lean on logic and critical thinking to break down a problem, question assumptions, and help the people I talk to see their situation more clearly. It's this pairing of heart and analysis that I bring to every psychology competition and every conversation with a friend who needs to think something through.
